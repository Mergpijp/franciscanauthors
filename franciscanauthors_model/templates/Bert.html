Bert Roest:

Between 1983-89 and 1991-92 I studied Intellectual History and Medieval Studies in Groningen and Toronto. This found its completion in the thesis Reading the Book of History. Intellectual Contexts and Educational Functions of Franciscan Historiography (Groningen: Regenboog, 1996). Since then, I was employed at various universities and research institutes in The Netherlands, Italy, and the USA. Between 1998 and 2001, I held a fellowship from the Dutch Royal Academy of Sciences (Koninklijke Nederlandse Akademie der Wetenschappen). Thereafter, I worked as an independent scholar, until I was offered a visiting professorship at St. Bonaventure University NY (The Doino Chair of Franciscan Studies) for the academic year 2003-2004. At present, I am active as a research consultant and as a lecturer of history at the University of Groningen.

Although most of my publications deal with the Franciscan order (men and women) during the later medieval period, I am also interested in other topics, notably the history of political thought until the present, philosophy of history, and paleo-anthropology. On top of that, I spend a lot of my free time restoring a 17th century farmhouse (made possible thanks to the help of a number of good friends).
