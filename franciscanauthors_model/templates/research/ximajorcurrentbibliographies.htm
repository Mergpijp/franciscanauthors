Deals with 29 authors, starting with Isabel de Villena (1430-1490) and ending with Diego Murillo (1555-1615). It deals at length with Alonso de Madrid, Bernardino de Laredo, Francisco de Osuna, Antonio de Guevara, Pedro de Alcántara, Diego de Estella, Juan de los Angeles, Iñigo de Mendoza, Ambrosio de Montesino, Juan de Cazalla, Bernabé de Palma, Miguel de Medina, Juan de Bonilla, Juan de Pineda, Melchor de Cetína, the afore-mentioned Isabel de Villena, Juan de la Cruz (not to be confused with the Mexican poet Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz, Francisco Tenorio, Francisco Hortiz, Luis de Maluenda, Francisco de Hevia, Juan de la Fuente, Nicolás Factor, Gabriel de Toro, Arcángel Alarcón de Tordesillas, Angel del Más de Perpiñan, Jerónimo de Segorbe, Diego Murillo and Pascual Baylón. Many articles of this massive volume provide lengthy passages and translations of source materials. See review by William J. Short in AFH 104 (2011), 331-334.

Francesco Neri, ‘I primi ottanta anni di ‘Italia Francescana’, Italia Francescana 81 (2006), 119-137.
